# Who We Are Image Generation Instructions

## Purpose
Generate 3 production-ready images to replace the placeholder cards in the Who We Are section.

The visuals should communicate:
- Structured strategy
- Professional production control
- Creative direction with clean delivery

These images are not decorative filler. They must reinforce the brand message: disciplined, premium, modern, and process-driven.

## Where These Images Are Used
Component context:
- Section title: Who We Are
- Core statement: Velris is a strategy-led creative partner for brands that have outgrown improvisation.
- Supporting message: structure, process, quality control, and accountable delivery

Current placeholder intents:
1. Strategy mapping board and campaign architecture references
2. Production quality controls
3. Creative direction and clean handover

## Brand Visual Direction
### Tone
- Premium
- Technical but human
- Controlled, not chaotic
- Confident, not flashy

### Styling
- Cinematic realism or polished editorial realism
- High detail, physically plausible lighting
- Professional environments (studio, strategy room, production workflow)
- No cartoon, no illustration, no 3D render look unless photoreal quality is guaranteed

### Color Language
Use restrained, dark-neutral bases with subtle accents inspired by site palette:
- Deep charcoal / black backgrounds
- Soft white highlights
- Controlled teal accents
- Sparse warm gold accents

Avoid oversaturated neon looks.

## Composition Rules
- Horizontal composition preferred
- Subject clarity at first glance, even when cropped
- Strong depth and layering
- Plenty of readable negative space
- No extreme fisheye distortion

### Framing Guidance per slot
- Image 01 is larger and more dominant
- Images 02 and 03 are secondary support visuals
- All three should feel like one coherent set

## Content Requirements
### Allowed Subjects
- Strategy walls, planning boards, campaign maps
- Professional camera/lighting setup, monitor checks, controlled production environments
- Creative direction moments: shot lists, color grading station, organized deliverables, file handoff context

### Forbidden Elements
- Visible brand logos (including Velris)
- Watermarks
- Random text overlays
- Meme aesthetics
- Fantasy/sci-fi motifs
- Stock-photo smiles and staged handshake tropes

### Human Presence
- Optional and minimal
- If people appear, keep them professional and secondary to process/workflow
- No identifiable faces in close-up unless requested

## Technical Output Specifications
Generate master assets with these settings:
- Format: PNG (preferred) or high-quality JPG
- Color space: sRGB
- Orientation: landscape
- Minimum size: 1920 x 1280
- Recommended master size: 2560 x 1707 or 2880 x 1920
- Sharpness: crisp mid-detail, avoid excessive artificial sharpening halos
- Compression artifacts: none visible

## Consistency Across the Set
For all 3 images, keep these shared constraints:
- Same general visual grade and contrast profile
- Similar lens language (roughly 28-50mm equivalent look)
- Similar lighting realism
- Unified mood: dark premium + controlled highlights

## Prompting Framework
Use this structure for each generation:
1. Subject and scene
2. Lighting and mood
3. Camera and composition
4. Material/detail realism cues
5. Style constraints
6. Negative constraints

## Master System Prompt (Reusable)
Use this as the main instruction to the image model:

"Create a photoreal, premium editorial-style image for a strategy-led creative agency website section. The scene must communicate structured execution, disciplined process, and professional quality control. Use realistic materials, cinematic but restrained lighting, and a dark-neutral palette with subtle teal and warm gold accents. Keep composition clean and legible for web hero/section cards, with strong depth and no visual clutter. Avoid logos, text overlays, watermarks, and gimmicky effects."

## Image-Specific Prompts

### Image 01: Strategy Architecture (Primary)
Intent: Communicate strategic planning and campaign architecture.

Prompt:
"Photoreal premium strategy workspace at night, campaign architecture board with organized references, timeline structure, color-coded planning markers, laptop showing structured project map, notebooks and production briefs arranged cleanly on a dark desk. Lighting is controlled and cinematic, with soft white practical highlights and subtle teal/gold accent reflections. Composition is wide horizontal, balanced, with clear negative space and depth layers, editorial realism, high detail, no logos, no text overlays."

Negative prompt:
"cartoon, illustration, low detail, blurry, oversaturated neon, random readable brand names, watermark, UI text overlays, chaotic clutter, smiling stock-photo team posing at camera"

### Image 02: Production Quality Control
Intent: Show technical precision and on-set control.

Prompt:
"Photoreal production monitoring setup in a controlled studio environment: professional camera rig, softbox lighting, reference monitor with waveform/vectorscope style visuals, checklist clipboard, organized cables and gear. Mood is focused and technical, dark-neutral environment, precise highlights, subtle teal accent illumination, premium documentary-editorial look. Horizontal composition, medium-wide framing, strong depth, clean professional order, no logos, no text overlays."

Negative prompt:
"messy amateur setup, toy-like camera gear, fantasy lighting, heavy lens flare, overexposed highlights, cartoon style, CGI look, watermark, random typography"

### Image 03: Creative Direction + Clean Handover
Intent: Communicate direction, post-production refinement, and organized delivery.

Prompt:
"Photoreal post-production and creative direction scene: calibrated monitor with cinematic frame preview, color grading controls, structured folder/handover checklist documents neatly arranged, external drives labeled with neutral non-branded tags, minimal but intentional desk styling. Mood is calm, precise, executive-ready, with restrained contrast and subtle warm-gold accents on dark surfaces. Horizontal composition, clean lines, high detail, premium realism, no logos, no text overlays."

Negative prompt:
"loud colorful gamer desk, RGB overload, cartoon render, unreadable noise, dirty lens effect, watermarks, branded logos, random poster text"

## Optional Variation Passes
Generate 3-5 variations per image using controlled variation:
- Keep core scene constant
- Adjust camera angle slightly
- Adjust lighting ratio subtly
- Keep color grade family consistent

Do not change overall narrative intent between variations.

## Quality Review Checklist
Approve only if all are true:
- Message clarity matches the assigned intent
- Looks premium and realistic on first view
- No logos/watermarks/text artifacts
- Cohesive as a 3-image set
- Works when viewed at smaller card sizes
- No distracting objects near image edges
- No uncanny anatomy if humans are present

## Cropping and Safe Areas
Because cards may crop differently:
- Keep critical subject matter inside the center 70% area
- Avoid placing key content on outer 10% margins
- Ensure each image still reads well in tighter crops

## File Naming Convention
Use this naming scheme:
- who-we-are-strategy-01.png
- who-we-are-production-02.png
- who-we-are-handover-03.png

Variation examples:
- who-we-are-strategy-01-v2.png
- who-we-are-production-02-v3.png

## Final Delivery Requirements
Deliver:
- 3 final selected images
- Optional 2 alternates per image
- Brief note on which variation is best for readability at small size
